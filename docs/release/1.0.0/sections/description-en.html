The Threat Intelligence Decision Ontology: Description back to ToC

The TIDO ontology can be used to describe the decision processes within the threat intelligence domain. As an example, we take a closer look at the beginning of the first episode of the 'de Dienst' podcast [17], where Liesbeth Rasker takes the role of an analyst at the Dutch General Intelligence and Security Service (AIVD) and is tasked with investigating a potential threat to the Dutch national security. In agreement with the creators of the podcast, we have published the transcript and english translation of the podcast in the `transcriptions` directory on our github page.

Step 1

The example starts with an orientation step (STEP 1), akin to the first phase of group decision making identified by Bales [6]. This step is about retrieving potentially relevant information for further analysis. In TIDO, this is modelled using the tido:wasRecalledBy relationship, where instances from the tido:PieceOfInformation class are recalled by a tido:Orientation step. In the current example, analyst Liesbeth recalls four pieces of information of the tido:Evidence class, which is modelled to be a subclass of tido:PieceOfInformation. Amongst these pieces of information, there is a the testimony from a concerned citizen, who noticed that a student in one of his classes was asking about substances that could be used to make a bomb (tido:e_4).

Example 1.1: 

@prefix xsd:  <http://www.w3.org/2001/XMLSchema#> .
@prefix prov: <http://www.w3.org/ns/prov#> .
@prefix tido: <http://w3id.org/tido> .
@prefix :     <http://example.org#> .

# Initialisation of the case investigation
:case_1
   a tido:Case.

# STEP 1
:orientation_1
   a tido:Orientation;
   tido:contributesTo  :case_1.

:e_1
   a tido:Evidence;
   tido:wasRecalledBy :orientation_1;
   rdf:value          "A report was received by the CISS front-office"@en.

:e_2
   a tido:Evidence;
   tido:wasRecalledBy :orientation_1;
   rdf:value          "The report was made by a concerned citizen"@en.

:e_3
   a tido:Evidence;
   tido:wasRecalledBy :orientation_1;
   rdf:value          "The concerned citizen teaches chemical engineering"@en.

:e_4
   a tido:Evidence;
   tido:wasRecalledBy :orientation_1;
   rdf:value          "During a practical, the concerned citizen noticed that one of his students was asking many questions about substances that could be used to create an explosive"@en.

Step 2

STEP 2 is the first of three tido:Evaluation steps. Here, the tido:Evaluation step is inspired by Bales' second phase of group decision making, where the facts of the situation and the proposed course of action are to being judged. Similar to the instances of the tido:Evidence class that were recalled during the :orientation_1 step, all instances of tido:RQ that are generated during the evaluation_1 step are linked to :case_1 through the prov:wasMemberOf relation. In this step, Liesbeth determines what is to be investigated, and makes this concrete by formalizing a research question :rq_1.

Example 1.2: 

# STEP 2
:evaluation_1
    a tido:Evaluation;
    tido:contributesTo :case_1;
    prov:wasInformedBy :orientation_1.

:rq_1
    a tido:RQ;
    prov:wasGeneratedBy :evaluation_1;
    rdf:value           "should we be concerned?"@en.

Step 3

In STEP 3 of the given example, referred to as :evaluation_2, Liesbeth drafts a hypothesis to answer the earlier derived research question rq_1, which is indicated using the tido:answerscode> relationship. Not only does Liesbeth generate :h_1, she also links three of the earlier identified potentially relevant pieces of information to this hypothesis using the tido:support relationship.

Example 1.3: 

# STEP 3
:evaluation_2
    a tido:Evaluation;
    tido:contributesTo :case_1;
    prov:wasInformedBy :evaluation_1.

:h_1
    a tido:Hypothesis;
    prov:wasGeneratedBy :evaluation_2;
    tido:answers        :rq_1;
    rdf:value           "There is not sufficient reason to be concerned"@en.

:h_2
    a tido:Hypothesis;
    prov:wasGeneratedBy :evaluation_2;
    tido:answers        :rq_1;
    rdf:value           "This situation could eventually lead to a cause of concern."@en.

:h_3
    a tido:Hypothesis;
    prov:wasGeneratedBy :evaluation_2;
    tido:answers        :rq_1;
    rdf:value           "There is a direct cause of concern"@en.

:e_2 tido:supports :h_2.
:e_3 tido:supports :h_2.
:e_4 tido:supports :h_2, :h_3.

Step 4

STEP 4 captures the process of identifying potential options for actions (i.e. :o_1), along several considerations (i.e. :c_1 and :c_2) associated with these options. In reality, multiple options will be identified and each will have a different, but potentially overlapping, set of considerations. As the tido:Consideration class is a subclass of tido:PieceOfInformation, the instances :c_1 and :c_2 can be used to either support, using tido:support, or dispute, using tido:dispute, the earlier derived hypothesis :h_1. In the current example, Liesbeth identifies the option to continue with the investigation, along with two additional considerations: one that supports hypothesis :h_1 and one that disputes it.

Example 1.4: 

# STEP 4
:evaluation_3 
    a tido:Evaluation;
    tido:contributesTo :case_1;
    prov:wasInformedBy :evaluation_2.

:c_1
    a tido:Consideration;
    prov:wasGeneratedBy :evaluation_3;
    tido:supports       :h_2;
    rdf:value           "It is weird that someone is orienting himself to build a bomb"@en.

:c_2
    a tido:Consideration;
    prov:wasGeneratedBy :evaluation_3;
    tido:supports       :h_3;
    rdf:value           "If you are following a degree in chemical engineering, you might just be interested in those kind of things"@en.

:o_1
    a tido:Option;
    prov:wasGeneratedBy   :evaluation_3;
    tido:hasConsideration :c_1, :c_2;
    tido:assumes          :h_2;
    rdf:value             "Continue with investigation"@en.

:o_2
    a tido:Option;
    prov:wasGeneratedBy   :evaluation_3;
    tido:hasConsideration :c_1, :c_2;
    tido:assumes          :h_3;
    rdf:value             "Wait until more reports come in"@en.

Step 5

In the last step, STEP 5, a tido:Resolution step is depicted which is analogous to Bales' third and final 'control' phase. The TIDO ontology makes no assumptions on the order of the steps within the processes, and therefore, the processes could in theory continue with another tido:Orientation, tido:Evaluation or tido:Resolution step. However, in this example the decision process is concluded with Liesbeth selecting the option to continue with the investigation, indicated by the tido:wasSelectedBy relation.

Example 1.5: 

# STEP 5
:resolution_1
    a tido:Resolution;
    tido:contributesTo :case_1;
    prov:wasInformedBy :evaluation_3.

:o_1 tido:wasSelectedBy :resolution_1.
